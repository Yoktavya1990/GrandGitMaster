📈 The $10 → $100 plan (compounding, not luck)

The whole idea: a digital product costs $0 to make and $0 to copy. So once you make your first $10, you don't spend it on living — you reinvest every dollar into more selling power until $100 comes on its own.

$0 → $10 — Make one thing, sell it 1–2 times. Use Win #1 or #2 above, or upload a mini-ebook (prompt #18) to Gumroad. Share the link in 3 places you already hang out (a group chat, a subreddit, your story). Goal: one or two sales.

$10 → $30 — Reinvest into a SECOND product + a review. Use your first $10 to buy one small promoted post OR a $5 Fiverr gig image for a better cover. Make a second product from the prompts. Two products = double the shots on goal.

$30 → $60 — Bundle & raise prices. Combine your products into a "bundle" priced higher than the parts. Ask every buyer for a testimonial — social proof lets you double your price honestly.

$60 → $100 — Put $20–30 into cheap, targeted promotion. A small boosted post or a shoutout in a niche group, pointing at your best-selling product. At this point each sale funds the next ad. That loop is the business.

Reality check: this is effort, not magic. Speed depends on your niche and how many people you show it to. The math only works if you actually ship and share — a product nobody sees earns $0. But the cost to try is genuinely zero.
